Timeline




Binary code
1689

The modern binary number system, the basis for binary code, was invented by Gottfried Leibniz and appears in his article Explication de l'Arithmétique Binaire. We know it as the zero’s and one’s that lay the foundation of computers.


SMGL
1960s

SGML descended from IBM's Generalized Markup Language (GML), which Charles Goldfarb, Edward Mosher, and Raymond Lorie developed in the 1960s. SGML was used at CERN, the European Organisation for nuclear research.


TimBL starts working at CERN
1980s

Tim Berners Lee, a computer scientist from England starts working at CERN. At the beginning of that decade, he started a personal project to get to grips with managing information. The resulting software was called ENQUIRE.


"Vague but exciting"
March 1989

Tim Berners Lee produced an unassuming document with the title “Information Management: A Proposal.” His supervisor, Mike Sendall gave permission by scrawling the words “vague but exciting” across the top of the paper.


HTML
19 november 1990

In 1989, Berners-Lee wrote a memo proposing an Internet-based hypertext system. Berners-Lee specified HTML and wrote the browser and server software in late 1990.


The first webpage
6 August 1991

The first web page went live on August 6, 1991. It was dedicated to information on the World Wide Web project and was made by Tim Berners-Lee. It ran on a NeXT computer at CERN.


CSS
10 October 1994

CSS was first proposed by Håkon Wium Lie on October 10, 1994. At the time, Lie was working with Tim Berners-Lee at CERN. Several other style sheet languages for the web were proposed around the same time.


iPhone
9 January 2007

The iPhone is the first smartphone model designed and marketed by Apple Inc.. After years of rumors and speculation, it was officially announced on January 9, 2007, and was later released in the United States on June 29, 2007.
